About Me

I am a doctoral candidate in the School of Teaching, Learning, and Curriculum Studies at Kent State University, where I serve as a Research Assistant at the KSU Child Development Center. My scholarship sits at the intersection of early childhood education policy, teacher preparation, and the experiences of underrepresented communities in early care and education settings.

My research employs mixed methods, qualitative analysis, and quantitative design to investigate the process and product of teacher recruitment, candidacy, and professional preparation — with a particular focus on ECE delivery among communities that have historically been marginalised in educational research and policy.

My work spans comparative contexts, with publications examining preschool environments in Nigeria, indigenous language acquisition, COVID-19's impact on youth learning, and the reconceptualisation of early childhood education policy in sub-Saharan Africa.
